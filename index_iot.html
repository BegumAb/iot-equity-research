06 · THE ONE ALPHA
Market View vs. My View

MARKET VIEW — “Samsara is fleet telematics.”

  • Features reach parity; Motive and Geotab compete on price.
  • Revenue growth fades 24% → 19.6% → 18.6%.
  • Multiple de-rated 13.7x → 9.8x NTM revenue.

MY VIEW — “Samsara is becoming an embedded physical-operations system.”

  • One install supports 5+ departments.
  • Multi-product expansion deepens the installed base.
  • Physical switching costs slow the fade.
“I do not need growth to avoid fading. I only need a slower fade.”
PROOFMy base case cuts NN ARR growth from +27% LTM to +15% / +5% — and still lands FY28 revenue 6.5% above Street.

08 · DEBATE 2
Commoditization & new logos — two separate battles
“If Motive is 30–40% cheaper, why would a new customer choose Samsara?”

INSTALLED BASE — switching cost protects existing customers

  • Hardware uninstall and reinstall are paid twice.
  • Deployment can take up to 12 months for a mid-size fleet.
  • Historical data and coaching records are difficult to transfer.
  • Insurance, safety, TMS and workflow integrations must be rebuilt.
  • A premium-paying customer renewed for another three years.

NEW LOGOS — ROI and platform breadth must earn the premium

  • Conceded: Motive’s 30–40% lower price is a real risk in compliance-only SMB.
  • 9 of top-10 new-logo deals included 2+ products; 4 of 10 included 4+.
  • Large new logos buy multi-product deployments out of the gate.
  • Insurance economics may narrow the effective price gap.
  • Workflow-heavy buyers value safety, maintenance, assets, integrations, one platform.

Q&A APPENDIX · PRESS N
Q&A — NAND Short Thesis
Not undiscovered; magnitude remains the debate. Concede the inflation — demand the bridge.

WHAT THE SHORT PROVES

  • NAND inflation is real. Contract prices: Q4 +33–38% · Q1 +55–60% · Q2 +70–75% (TrendForce).
  • Component prices moved sharply — an industry-wide memory cost cycle, not a Samsara-specific event.
  • Hardware cost risk exists. Devices carry memory; visibility on supply shortened to a couple of quarters (CFO, June).
Conceded in full. I do not argue NAND is harmless.

WHAT HE HAS NOT PROVEN

  • Samsara’s realized cost — contracted and pre-bought, not the spot quote.
  • Memory’s share of the device BOM — undisclosed by Samsara and by every peer (verified).
  • Affected device volume shipping at new pricing.
  • Timing through 5-year amortization — the P&L sees a phased drip, blended with cheaper vintages.
  • A margin / EPS miss beyond guidance — asked in March & June; built into flat-GM assumptions and ~100bps FY27 FCF-margin leverage; sell-side already models no GM expansion.
My net sensitivity (Memory Offset tab): base ≈ flat · high ≈ −55bps · stress ≈ −100bps.
VERDICTIndustry risk, not yet company-specific alpha. The bridge question stands: walk from the $25 part quote to the FY27 GM and EPS miss — part spec, BOM share, realized price, unit volume, amortization. Graded September 3.
